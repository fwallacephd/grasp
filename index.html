About Us

GRASP was founded by Drs. Ruby and Wallace, two reading specialists with expertise in assessment and diagonstics of readers, K-12. Through GRASP, they work with students of all ages and abilities to understand their individual reading needs and create a report of their findings with recommendations for instruction that parents can use as a basis for hiring tutors or take to school for parent-teacher conferences. Drs. Ruby and Wallace do offer tutoring services on a limited basis.

Our Experience

Drs. Ruby and Wallace met during their doctoral program back in 2000 at Georgia State University. There, they specialized in langauge and literacy acquisition, specifically focusing on assessment of individual learners, K -12. After their doctoral program, both Drs. Ruby and Wallace went on to become Professors of Reading and taught future reading teachers best practices in reading assessment and instruction. Now, they are pooling their knowledge and embarking on a new endeaver, to work directly with the students of Georiga to assess their individual learning needs and advocate for their instruction.


Services

Are you worried that your son or daughter is lagging behind in reading? Do they dislike reading or have a hard time finding the right book? Are you worried that your son or daughter is reading above grade level and is bored in school? At GRASP, we work with all readers from K - 12, whether they are gifted or remedial or somewhere in between, to understand their unique reading abilities and develop a plan of recommendations to fit their individual learning needs.


Basic

Assessment always begins with building rapport between the facilitator and the student. We start with a reading interview to understand reading preferences, strategies for choosing books, and goals as a reader. Then, we use leveled-word lists and passages to determine individual strengths and weaknesses in the following categories: phonemic awareness and phonics (when applicable), word recognition, comprehension, fluency, and vocabulary. A detailed report of our findings, recommendations for instruciton and follow-up conference is included.
